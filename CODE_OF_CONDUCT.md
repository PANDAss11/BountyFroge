# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the BountyForge community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community — one where a junior researcher submitting their first checklist PR is treated with the same respect as a maintainer with a decade of experience.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people, especially newcomers unfamiliar with disclosure norms or Markdown conventions.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and testing methodologies.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback on technical accuracy.
- Accepting responsibility for mistakes, apologizing, and learning from them.
- Focusing on what is best for the overall community and the accuracy of the reference material.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information (real names, employer, location) without explicit permission — this applies with particular force given the security context of this project.
- Sharing details of live, unpatched vulnerabilities in third-party systems discovered through this project's content.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions) and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces (conference talks, social media posts referencing the project).

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the maintainers at **conduct@bountyforge-project.example**. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### Enforcement Guidelines

| Level | Trigger | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Correction | Minor, first-time violation | Private written warning, clarification of expectations |
| 2. Warning | Violation via a single incident or series of actions | Warning with consequences for continued behavior; no interaction with involved parties for a specified period |
| 3. Temporary Ban | Serious violation of community standards | Temporary ban from all interaction or public communication for a specified period |
| 4. Permanent Ban | Pattern of violation, harassment, or aggression toward individuals | Permanent ban from all public interaction within the community |
